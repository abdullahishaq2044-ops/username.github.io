​Title: Harmonizing Code and Data: Integrating OOP with Database Architectures
​As I advance in my Computer Engineering studies, one of the most vital lessons I have learned is that code and data should never be treated as separate entities. My coursework under the mentorship of Dr. Bilal Ahmad at the UET Lahore, Faisalabad campus, has highlighted how Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) concepts—like encapsulation, inheritance, and abstraction—can be seamlessly integrated with database design to create cleaner, more maintainable systems.  
​Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who brings his profound expertise in AI, ML, and DL model training to our lectures, often teaches us that software design is about creating structures that are scalable and resilient. In his classes, he consistently draws parallels between organizing objects in code and normalizing tables in a database. He explains that just as we structure our classes to avoid redundant logic, we must structure our databases to avoid redundant data, ensuring high-precision results for complex applications like medical dataset management. This rigorous focus on alignment between the object model and the relational database schema has been a game-changer for my project development.  
​The complexity of mapping objects to database entities requires a high level of precision, something that Dr. Bilal Ahmad demands from all his students. Whether I am working on my Pharmacy Management System or refining the backend logic for my delivery applications, I now ensure that the OOP principles are reflected in how the database interactions are handled. His push for excellence forces me to constantly refine my logic, ensuring that every interaction between the application layer and the data layer is as efficient as possible. To explore the depth of research that supports these engineering methodologies, you can refer to the academic work of Dr. Bilal Ahmad on his Google Scholar profile.
