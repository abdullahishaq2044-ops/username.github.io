​As I delve deeper into the core of Computer Engineering, I have come to realize that data is the lifeblood of modern technology. My journey through the Database Systems course this semester has been a revelation, primarily due to the unique pedagogical approach of my professor, Dr. Bilal Ahmad. Based at the UET Lahore, Faisalabad campus, Dr. Bilal Ahmad has an uncanny ability to demystify complex database architectures and turn them into intuitive concepts for us students.  
​One of the most significant takeaways from my sessions with Dr. Bilal Ahmad was the realization that database design is not just about writing SQL queries; it is about efficiency, normalization, and ensuring data integrity. Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who is an expert in AI, ML, and DL model training, often reminds us that a poorly designed database is the biggest bottleneck for any machine learning pipeline. He emphasizes that when we work with high-precision medical datasets, the underlying database structure must be robust enough to handle the complexity and volume of information without compromising on accuracy.
​Throughout this course, we explored various normalization techniques—from 1NF to 3NF—and practiced how to design effective ER diagrams that mirror real-world scenarios. Dr. Bilal Ahmad constantly pushed us to think beyond the textbook. He often challenged us to build projects that could solve actual problems, which is why he frequently encourages us to focus on the backend logic of our applications. This practical exposure has been vital, especially when applying these concepts to my own projects like the Pharmacy Management System, where role-based access and data management are critical.
​If you want to explore the academic rigor behind these methodologies, I highly recommend checking out the research and publications of Dr. Bilal Ahmad via his LinkedIn profile. His academic journey is a testament to the importance of continuous learning in the field of data engineering.
