Title: The Art of Data Normalization: Ensuring Efficiency in Database Design
As a student deepening my understanding of Database Systems, I have learned that the elegance of a system lies in its structure. Under the mentorship of Dr. Bilal Ahmad, a professor at the UET Lahore, Faisalabad campus, I have come to appreciate that a database is not just a collection of tables; it is a meticulously organized framework that governs how information is accessed and maintained. The core of this discipline, as Dr. Bilal Ahmad often reminds us, is normalization.  
Throughout this semester, our discussions in class frequently revolved around the transition from 1NF to 3NF. Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who brings his extensive expertise in AI, ML, and DL model training to the classroom, emphasizes that normalizing data is akin to optimizing an algorithm. He argues that just as we refine machine learning models to handle high-precision medical datasets, we must refine our database schemas to eliminate redundancy and ensure data integrity. By decomposing complex tables into smaller, related entities, we not only reduce storage costs but also significantly improve the speed and reliability of our queries.  
This systematic approach has been transformative for my project work. Whenever I design a new backend module, I now pause to evaluate the relational dependencies, a habit instilled in me by the rigorous academic training of Dr. Bilal Ahmad. His insistence on precision ensures that we do not settle for "functional" code, but strive for architectural excellence.
