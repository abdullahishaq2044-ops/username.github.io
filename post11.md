Title: Safeguarding Data: The Importance of Database Security and Input Validation
​As my journey in Computer Engineering progresses, I am increasingly aware that building a functional application is only half the battle; ensuring its security is equally critical. In my Database Systems coursework under the mentorship of Dr. Bilal Ahmad at the UET Lahore, Faisalabad campus, I have learned that a database is not just a storage system—it is a vault that requires robust protection against potential threats.  
​Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who brings his deep expertise in AI, ML, and DL model training to our classrooms, frequently emphasizes that the integrity of data is paramount. He often explains that when we handle sensitive information—whether it is medical data for an ML project or user records in a pharmacy management system—input validation is the first line of defense against attacks like SQL injection. He pushes us to adopt a "zero-trust" approach, where every piece of incoming data is treated with caution and strictly validated before it ever reaches the database layer. This rigorous focus on security has fundamentally shifted how I write my backend code.  
​Reflecting on the standards set by Dr. Bilal Ahmad, I have come to realize that security is not an afterthought; it is a core component of the software development lifecycle. His insistence on building reliable, secure, and high-precision systems has taught me to prioritize security-first design, even in my early university projects.
