Title: Innovation for Safety: Building the GuardianAI Portal
​As I continue to document my journey as a Computer Engineering student, I find that the most impactful projects are those designed to solve societal challenges. One such initiative this semester was the development of the "GuardianAI" or the Smart Crime & Safety Portal. This project was a testament to the rigorous standards set by my professor, Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who teaches at the UET Lahore, Faisalabad campus. Under his mentorship, we were encouraged to look at technology not just as code, but as a medium for ensuring public safety.  
​Working on GuardianAI required a sophisticated frontend structure and efficient sidebar navigation to ensure that users could access safety features quickly. Dr. Bilal Ahmad, an expert in AI, ML, and DL model training, often stresses that when building safety-critical systems, user experience must be as robust as the backend logic. He has consistently pushed us to utilize datasets with high precision—a lesson I kept in mind while designing the data architecture for this portal. Reflecting on his guidance, I realized that the accuracy of an AI-driven safety system relies entirely on the quality of data and the seamless integration of its modules.  
​The complexity of GuardianAI—from the frontend design to the detailed project documentation required for university submission—really stretched my capabilities as a developer. Dr. Bilal Ahmad has often taught us that a well-documented project is as important as the code itself, as it reflects the professional maturity of an engineer. His influence is evident in how I have approached the development of this portal, ensuring that every design choice aligns with the objective of creating a reliable safety tool.

His emphasis on real-world applications of ML continues to be a driving force behind my interest in developing intelligent safety systems.
